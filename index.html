Emotional Identification and Tweaking of Policy-Controlled Physics Enabled Virtual Characters

Project for the acquisition of a Masters Degree in Computer Science and Engineering

Diogo Silva

Abstract

The automatic generation of motion through Reinforcement Learning techniques has been a hot-topic of discussion and investigation. Recent advancements made in this field have led to the creation of robust systems capable of learning to imitate given reference animations. Some of these systems go a step beyond, allowing for style exploration at the time of learning, providing an avenue to curate the outcome animation's expressiveness. There haven't, however, been many studies made on how to allow users to edit a character's expressed emotion in real time after the motion learning process. By combining Deep Reinforcement Learning for the learning of the base animation with Machine Learning modules based around Emotion Analysis - in particular Laban Movement Analysis and the Pleasure,Arousal, Dominance Emotion State Model - we have developed a system capable of identifying the perceived emotion of both Kinematic and Policy Controlled Physics-Based virtual characters focused on locomotion that also allows users to alter the expressed emotion, in real time, without further training.
